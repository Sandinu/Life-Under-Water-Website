Reduce marine pollution

Reducing marine pollution entails implementing strategies to minimize pollutants entering oceans. This involves better waste management, reducing plastic use, stricter regulations on runoff, sustainable fishing, and raising awareness. These actions protect marine ecosystems, human health, and ensure the health of our planet's oceans for future generations.

Protect and restore ecosystems

Protecting and restoring ecosystems involves safeguarding natural habitats, conserving biodiversity, and rehabilitating degraded environments. This includes initiatives like preserving forests, wetlands, and coral reefs, as well as restoring degraded lands through reforestation and sustainable land management practices. These efforts promote resilience to environmental challenges and enhance ecosystem services, benefiting both nature and humanity.

Reduce Ocean Acidification

Reducing ocean acidification involves mitigating the increase in acidity levels caused by CO2 absorption. This is achieved through measures like reducing CO2 emissions, promoting renewable energy, and protecting vulnerable marine ecosystems. By addressing the root causes, we preserve marine biodiversity and protect coastal communities reliant on healthy oceans.

Sustainable Fishing

Sustainable fishing involves practices that maintain fish populations, marine ecosystems, and support fishing communities. It aims to prevent overfishing, minimize bycatch, and adhere to regulations while ensuring social and economic fairness. By promoting sustainable fishing, we protect marine biodiversity, secure food sources, and sustain livelihoods for present and future generations.

Conserve coastal and marine areas

Conserving coastal and marine areas involves protecting and managing these ecosystems to sustain biodiversity and ecological health. Efforts focus on preserving habitats, reducing pollution, regulating human activities, and promoting sustainable resource use. Through conservation, we safeguard critical habitats, protect endangered species, and ensure the long-term benefits these areas provide for ecosystems and human communities.

End subsidies contrubuting to overfishing

Ending subsidies contributing to overfishing entails discontinuing financial incentives that promote unsustainable fishing practices. By eliminating these subsidies, governments can encourage sustainable fishing, reduce overcapacity in fishing fleets, and restore fish stocks. This action is crucial for safeguarding marine ecosystems, supporting fisheries-dependant communities, and achieving sustainable develpoment goals related to oceans and fisheries management.

Increase the economic benefits from sustainable use of marine resources

INcreasingeconomic benifits from sustainable use of marine resources involves maximizing the value of activities like fishing and tourism while ensuring environmental responsibility. This includes promoting sustainable practices in fishing, investing in eco-friendly aquaculture, and developing ecotourism initiatives. By prioratizing sustainability, stakeholders can unlock long-term economic opportunities while protecting marine ecosystems.

Increase scientific knowledge, research, and technology for ocean health

Increasing scientific knowledge, research, and technology for ocean health involves investing in understanding ocean ecosystems, biodiversity, and human impacts. This includes studying pollution, climate change, and overfishing to promote sustainable ocean stewardship and protect marine systems for future genarations.

Support small scale fishers

Supporting small-scale fishers entails implementing policies and progams that prioritize their needs and rights. This includes providing access to resources, fair prices for their catch, and involving them in decision-making. Empowering small-scale fishers promotes sustainable practices, alleviates poverty, and enhances food security for coastal communities.

Implement and enforce internatinal sea law

Implementing and enforcing international sea law ensures adherence to regulations governing ocean activities, including maritime boundaries, navigation, and environmental protection. Upholding these laws promotes peaceful relations, prevents conflicts over resources, and facilities sustainable ocean use for the benefit of all.
